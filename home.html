MY WORK

A lesson everyday

With every new piece of work, I find myself delving into something I've never tried before. Whether it's a way of doing something, a new something entirely, or some combination of the two, my skillset is scarcely static. This evolution of course leads to looking back on my previous work with a feeling akin to horror, but I can also use this reflection to appreciate how much I have learned in that time.

Below is a selection of video projects I have undertaken in the past few years. Paired with each video will be a short description of what I learned doing it.

VIDEO


My first recipe video with the Marketing Association. I learned how to create a storyboard, the excitement of sound design, and to keep my lighting out of the shot. Oops.



My next foray into food videos, this time outdoors! In this video I learned about masking transitions, how much slow-mo is too much, and how much flexibility nesting clips allows.



Expanding on my knowledge of masking transitions, the opening seconds of this video remain high on my list of my favorite work. Unfortunately, after that I feel I lost direction. The remaining shots and transitions don't share the same level of planning, and could have used significantly more forethought.



More experimentation with in-camera transitions. Trying to pair motions together to make videos feel seamless.



This video, filmed at the same time as the previous, was more impromptu. We had the salmon for another recipe, so we decided to whip this one up. I like the homey, simple feel of this video, even if there's nothing particularly stunning about the editing.



The most difficult part of this video was masking multiple video layers on to text and getting them to all line up properly. Painstaking is an understatement. When masked, there are 4 separate areas to change position of the layers - 3 work for the text itself, and 1 works for the background video. Which is which? Only experience can tell!



If these videos were in true chronological order, this would be higher on this page. Voiced by my co-worker Annalee, edited by myself, this video was a great practice in reusing footage. At this time, we weren't in a position to capture any fresh video content, but we needed this video assembled. I went through our old footage library and chopped up various videos & fisherman audio clips to assemble this piece.



A relatively straight-forward video, put together in an attempt to alleviate the impact of our absence at Seafood Expo North America 2021. I look back on this and wish I'd done a better job lighting Christa, but I can excuse it for being my first time filming anything remotely interview-style.



A more recent video, once again largely unplanned. Over this past summer, we worked frequently with the chefs at Orca Lodge, here in Cordova. We gave them each a fillet of salmon and asked them to prepare a dish, while I did my best to follow along with the camera. With unplanned shots, assembly is, once again, a matter of experimentation.



Similar to the previous, this video was entirely unplanned. My biggest improvement over the last video was to film with my tripod attached to the camera, giving myself a big more weight to reduce camera-shake while shooting hand-held.

Oftentimes, my inspiration comes from a piece of music. The night before I made this video, I heard this song and was filled with emotion - I knew I needed to design something around the dark, somber tone this song delivers. I also took this as an opportunity to try out some new transition effects and color-grading styles, and am largely happy with the result.
